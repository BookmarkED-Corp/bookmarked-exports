Library Voices — Email Templates

Four ready-to-send templates for the community content program. Personalize the placeholders and send.

1. Warm Invitation

First Touch
From: Whoever has the relationship (Steve, Patrick, Jan, or Teela)
When: After identifying a good candidate

Subject: Something we're starting that I think you'd love

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to reach out about something we're putting together called Library Voices — a program where we spotlight librarians and district leaders who are doing incredible work in their school libraries.

The idea is simple: give people like you a platform to talk about what you care about. The future of libraries, getting books in kids' hands, the innovative things you're doing at [District Name] — whatever matters most to you.

This isn't a product testimonial. It's about celebrating the profession and the people in it. We'd love for you to be one of our first featured voices.

There are a few ways to participate — from a quick 5-minute quote to a longer written piece or even a webinar panel. I can send you the full menu if you're interested.

Would you be open to it? No pressure at all — just thought of you because [personal reason — e.g., "of the amazing things you shared about your reading program last month"].

Steve Wandler
Founder, Bookmarked
steve@bookmarked.com
(214) 892-1015
Usage notes: Replace the signature with your own if you're not Steve. The [personal reason] is critical — it should reference something specific about them or their library. Generic invites don't work.

2. Program Overview + Form Link

After Opt-In
From: Same person who sent the invitation
When: After they express interest

Subject: Library Voices — here's how it works

Hi [First Name],

So glad you're interested! Here's a quick overview of Library Voices and how you can participate.

The idea: We want to give librarians and district leaders a platform to talk about what matters — the future of school libraries, innovative approaches to getting books in students' hands, and the amazing work happening in libraries across Texas.

How you can participate:

  • Quick Quote (5 min) — Answer 2-3 questions via email. We feature you on social media.
  • Photo Spotlight (15 min) — Send a photo of yourself in your library + answer 3 short questions.
  • Written Piece (30-60 min) — Write about a topic you care about, or we interview you and write it up.
  • Webinar Panel (60 min) — Join a recorded conversation with peers on a topic that matters.
  • Case Study (45 min) — Share your experience for a co-branded PDF you can use with your admin.

Pick whatever feels right for you — or multiple options! Just fill out this short form and we'll take it from there:

Join Library Voices

We'll share a draft of everything before it goes live. Nothing gets published without your approval.

Thanks for being part of this, [First Name].

Steve Wandler
Founder, Bookmarked
steve@bookmarked.com
(214) 892-1015
Usage notes: Replace the form link with the actual HubSpot form URL once created. Update HubSpot contact status to "Opted In" when this email is sent.

3. Quick Quote Request

Direct Ask
From: Whoever has the relationship
When: For fast testimonials or when they chose "Quick Quote"

Subject: Quick favor — 3 questions for Library Voices

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for being part of Library Voices! For your spotlight, I just need your answers to three short questions. Reply to this email with whatever comes to mind — no need to overthink it.

1. What do you love most about being a school librarian?

2. What's one thing you wish people understood about school libraries today?

3. What excites you about the future of libraries?

We'll lightly edit for clarity and send you a draft before anything goes live. Your voice, your approval.

If you have a favorite photo of yourself in your library, send that along too — we'd love to include it in the spotlight!

Steve Wandler
Founder, Bookmarked
steve@bookmarked.com
(214) 892-1015
Usage notes: You can swap the questions from the Question Bank in the program doc. Pick questions that match what you know about this person — if they're passionate about innovation, use the innovation questions. If they love kids and reading, use the vitality questions.
