Program Design

Four Pillars

Library Voices is built on four pillars — each creates different content types and serves different strategic goals.

Pillar 1

Librarian Spotlights

Celebrate individual librarians and the amazing work they do. Photo + quote + short profile. Published biweekly on Instagram and LinkedIn.

Content: About THEM, not about Bookmarked

Pillar 2

Thought Leadership

Written pieces and blog posts on topics librarians care about: the future of libraries, getting books in kids' hands, innovative approaches to collection management, AI and libraries.

Content: Their voice, our platform

Pillar 3

ESC Regional Stories

Feature districts within ESC partner regions. Give partners concrete content to share with their member districts — proof that Bookmarked works in their backyard.

Content: Regional credibility for partners

Pillar 4

Conversations (Webinars)

Quarterly recorded panels with 2-3 librarians discussing topics that matter. Not product demos — real conversations about the profession and its future. Launch in 2-3 months.

Content: Community building at scale

Content Philosophy

Library-First, Positive, Future-Focused

This program is NOT about testimonials for our sales page. It's about giving librarians and district leaders a platform to talk about what matters to them — and letting Bookmarked be the brand that made that platform possible.

📚

Library Vitality

How do we create vibrant libraries where kids are asking and wanting to check out books? What makes a library the best room in a school?

🌱

Innovation

What are librarians doing to get books in kids' hands in new and creative ways? How are districts rethinking collection management?

🚀

The Future

How do libraries evolve in a world of AI and digital content? What does the next generation of school libraries look like?

Hard Boundaries

  • Never position Bookmarked as replacing librarians — "We give librarians their time back"
  • Never use fear-based framing — lead with gains, not threats
  • Spotlights are about the PERSON and the PROFESSION, not our product
  • Nothing published without the customer's explicit approval
  • Never cross district data boundaries
  • ESCs are partners in this conversation — they have a voice too

Operations

Collection Workflow

A repeatable process anyone on the team can run — Jan, Patrick, Teela, or Steve. Same structure, same quality, every time.

Who Asks & When

Who Can Invite

Steve, Patrick, Jan, Teela — anyone with a customer relationship. The process is designed so the infrastructure handles the work, not the person asking.

When to Ask

  • After successful onboarding (Arrows plan 80%+ complete)
  • After a positive support interaction
  • After a renewal
  • When a librarian shares something great organically
  • When someone hears a great quote in conversation

The Three Paths

Different relationships call for different approaches. Use the path that fits.

Path A

Direct Form

For warm relationships. Send a branded email with a link to the intake form. They fill it out, we produce the content.

Best for: Established customers who'd say yes immediately

Path B

Menu First

Send the participation overview first. They choose what interests them, then get the specific form for that content type.

Best for: Newer relationships, people who need to see options

Path C

Conversation First

Talk about it on a call. Gauge interest, discuss what would work for them, then send the follow-up form to collect specifics.

Best for: Key accounts, people who prefer a personal touch

Regional Strategy

ESC Partner Integration

Every district story is also a regional story. When a district participates, their ESC partner gets content they can use to build credibility in their region.

What ESC Partners Receive

  • Social-ready graphic + quote card (co-branded)
  • Short write-up for their newsletter
  • Talking point: "Here's what [District] in your region is doing"
  • Optional: Video clip for conferences and PD sessions

ESCs as Content Creators Too

ESC partners aren't just amplifiers — they have their own perspective on what libraries of the future look like and how Bookmarked helps districts in this new world. They can participate in Library Voices directly.

  • ESC director spotlight on their vision for libraries
  • Webinar panelist representing the regional perspective
  • Co-authored thought piece on PD and library support

Regional Coverage Goal

At least one featured district per ESC partner region per quarter. Track coverage in the Confluence hub — identify which regions have stories and which need outreach.

Phase 2 — Launch in 2-3 Months

Webinar Series: "Library Conversations"

Quarterly recorded panels. Real conversations about libraries, not product demos. Start identifying interested panelists now, plan the first webinar 2-3 months out.

Format

  • 30-minute recorded panel
  • 2-3 librarians/district leaders + Steve or Patrick as moderator
  • Topics driven by community interest (poll participants)
  • Recorded via Zoom, posted to YouTube
  • Repurposed into 4-6 social clips
  • No hard sell — pure thought leadership
