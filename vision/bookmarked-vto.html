Marketing Strategy

Target Market

  • K-12 school districts in states with book compliance legislation (26 states today, projected 35-40 by 2029)
  • Sweet spot: 10,000-50,000 students
  • Primary buyer: Superintendents and Directors of Library Services
  • Key insight: Many districts THINK they're compliant but are spending enormous time on manual processes. It is humanly impossible for librarians to review 10,000+ books with fidelity using current tools.

Three Uniques

1. Proactive intelligence that never sleeps

We continuously scan the web for flags — news, challenges, watchlists, reviews, legislative changes — and surface them before they become crises.

2. Legally defensible by design

Every action creates an audit trail. When a book is challenged, the district has documentation of their review process, the evidence considered, and the rationale for their decision. This isn't a feature we added — it's how the system was built.

3. We see the entire district — and optimize it

Not just the library catalog. Classroom libraries, supplemental materials, the books districts don't know they have. 40% of library books are dead inventory. We make the invisible visible.

Decision Framework

Trade-Off Hierarchy

When two good things compete, the hierarchy decides. Never sacrifice a higher tier for a lower one.

Tier 1 — Stakeholder Trust (never sacrifice)

Customer trust & relationship quality > Short-term efficiency. Districts in ESC networks talk. One burned relationship radiates through the network.

Data accuracy > Speed of delivery. Bookmarked's product IS trusted data. Wrong compliance data = board-level liability for districts.

Tier 2 — Business Sustainability

Retention of existing customers > Acquisition of new ones. Churned districts signal to ESC peers. Retention is reputation. NRR drives enterprise value.

Tier 3 — Organizational Character

Building systems that scale > Achieving one-time goals. Systems compound. Goals complete. Prefer the flywheel over the tugboat.

Care Enough to Be Honest > Avoiding difficult conversations. Surface problems early.

Focus Over Frenzy > Responding to every opportunity. Say no to protect the yes.

The Reversibility Rule

Reversible decisions → act fast. Speed matters more than perfection when you can undo it.

Irreversible decisions → escalate. Slow down, get input, verify alignment.

Unsure which? → treat as irreversible.
