EOS: The 6 Components

A simple operating system for running a business. One page. Plain language.

EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System) says every business has six components. When all six are strong, the business runs well. When one is weak, you feel it everywhere. Most companies have the first one figured out. Most are missing the last one. Here are all six.
1
Vision
Where are we going and how do we plan to get there?
The leadership team must be 100% on the same page about where the company is going. Not 80%. Not "roughly aligned." 100%. This means answering 8 questions: What are our core values? What's our core focus? What's our 10-year target? What does 3 years look like? What's the 1-year plan? What are our quarterly priorities? What's our marketing strategy? What are the issues we need to solve?

The tool for this is the V/TO (Vision/Traction Organizer) — one document that captures all 8 answers.

2
People
Do we have the right people in the right seats?
Two tests. Right person = they share your core values. Use the People Analyzer: rate every person +, +/−, or − on each value. Three minuses = wrong person, no matter how talented. Right seat = they GWC their role. Get it (they understand the job). Want it (they genuinely want to do it). Capacity to do it (they have the skills and time).

The tool is the Accountability Chart — one function per seat, one person per seat, every seat has 5 major roles defined. Not an org chart — an accountability chart. If nobody owns it, nobody does it.

3
Data
Do we have a pulse on the business based on facts, not feelings?
Run the business on a handful of numbers, not opinions. Every person has a number they own. Every week, that number is either on track or off track. No stories. No excuses. Green or red.

The tool is the Scorecard — 5–15 weekly activity-based numbers that tell leadership the health of the business at a glance. If a number is off track, it goes on the Issues List and gets solved.

4
Issues
Are we good at solving problems as they come up?
Every company has issues. Strong companies solve them quickly instead of talking about them endlessly. Maintain one open list of everything in the way. Don't hide problems. Don't let them fester. Surface them, solve them, move on.

The tool is IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve). Identify the real root cause (not the symptom). Discuss it (everyone shares their view once — no repeating). Solve it (a to-do with an owner and a deadline). Takes 10 minutes per issue. Most meetings spend 60 minutes discussing and 0 minutes solving. IDS flips that.

5
Process
Do we have a consistent way of doing our core things?
Identify the handful of core processes that run the business — how you sell, how you onboard, how you build product, how you support customers. Document each one in simple steps. Not a 50-page manual. Just the 10–20 key steps so everyone does it the same way, every time.

The test: if the person who runs it got hit by a bus tomorrow, could someone else follow the steps? Process is how you scale without chaos.

6
Traction
Are we actually executing — doing what we said we'd do?
This is where most companies fall apart. The vision is great. The people are talented. But nothing happens consistently. Traction is discipline. Two tools make it work:

Rocks — 3 to 7 company priorities per quarter. Each one is owned by one person. Each has a clear definition of "done." At the end of 90 days, it's either done or it's not. No partial credit.

The Level 10 Meeting (L10) — a weekly meeting with the same agenda, same day, same time, every single week. Check-in. Scorecard review. Rock review. To-do review. Then spend the rest of the time solving issues with IDS. Rate the meeting 1–10 at the end. Ends on time even if you're not done. This is the heartbeat of the company.

To-Dos — every action item from the L10 has an owner and is due within 7 days. Done or not done. Target: 90% completion rate. If it's not getting done, it's an issue — IDS it.

How the 6 Components Work Together

Vision tells everyone where we're going. People puts the right humans in the right seats to get there. Data gives us a weekly pulse so we know if we're on track. Issues gives us a way to solve what's in the way. Process makes sure we do things consistently. Traction makes sure we actually do what we said we'd do.

The L10 meeting is where it all comes together every week. That's where the Scorecard gets reviewed, Rocks get checked, issues get solved, and to-dos get assigned. Miss the L10, and the whole system breaks.
