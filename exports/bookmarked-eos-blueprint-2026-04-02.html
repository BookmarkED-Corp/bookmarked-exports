Why We're Doing This

Bookmarked has vision, product, and momentum. What we lack is consistent operational discipline — the structure that turns vision into weekly execution, makes expectations clear for everyone, and holds the entire team (including the CEO) accountable to measurable outcomes.

EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System) gives us the framework. We're not adopting it because it's trendy — we're adopting it because we need one system, one language, one rhythm that everyone follows. No exceptions. No excuses. Results over reasons.

This is not a document to read and file. This is how we operate, starting now.

2. Core Values

These are discovered from our best people, not invented. Every hire, fire, review, and recognition decision runs through these values. The test: Would you fire someone who consistently violated this value, even if they were hitting their numbers?

Own It Build With What You Have Never Stop Learning Care Enough to Be Honest Focus Over Frenzy
Own It
Take initiative. Solve problems. Don't wait to be told. See what needs to happen and make it happen — before anyone asks.
Build With What You Have
Resourcefulness over resources. Work within what's available and build from there. Don't wait for perfect conditions. Create structure for others to follow.
Never Stop Learning
Always be growing. Elevate yourself and everything around you. Create A-ha moments. Complacency is the enemy.
Care Enough to Be Honest
People matter. Truth matters. Deliver both with respect. Hard truths delivered with understanding — without undermining how others think.
Focus Over Frenzy
When there are a hundred ideas flying, pick the right ones. Execute with discipline. "How about we do this and talk about the other things later?" Alignment with the vision beats activity without direction.

How We Use Core Values

  • People Analyzer (+/+/-/-): Rate every team member on each value quarterly
  • Hiring: Screen for values fit before evaluating skills
  • Firing: Three or more minuses on Core Values = wrong person, regardless of performance
  • Quarterly Conversations: Core Values review is Part 1 of the 5-5-5
  • Recognition: Thump-Thump moments tied to specific Core Values

7. IDS — Identify, Discuss, Solve

The EOS process for resolving issues. Most teams discuss issues endlessly. We solve them — with a to-do, an owner, and a deadline.

I
Identify
Name the real issue. Not the symptom. Get to the root cause in one sentence.
D
Discuss
Everyone shares their perspective once. No repeating. 10-minute max per issue. Stay on root cause.
S
Solve
Land on a to-do with an owner and a deadline. If it can't be solved in 10 minutes, break it into a smaller solve + a follow-up.

8. Trade-Off Hierarchy

Core Values tell us who we are. The Trade-Off Hierarchy tells us what happens when those values pull in different directions. Never sacrifice a higher tier for a lower one.

Tier 1
Stakeholder Trust (never sacrifice — this is our moat)
Customer trust > short-term efficiency. Data accuracy > speed of delivery.
Districts in ESC networks talk. One burned relationship radiates through the network.
Tier 2
Business Sustainability
Retention of existing customers > acquisition of new ones.
Churned districts signal to ESC peers. NRR drives enterprise value.
Tier 3
Organizational Character
Systems that scale > one-time goals. Honesty > avoiding hard conversations. Focus > responding to every opportunity.

Hard Boundaries — Never Cross

  • Never position Bookmarked as replacing librarians
  • Never use fear-based framing with customers
  • Never commit to timelines without engineering input
  • Never share customer data across district boundaries
  • Never send investor communications without Steve's review
  • Never auto-approve financial commitments above $5,000

9. What Makes This AI-Native

Bookmarked is an AI-native company. That means AI agents aren't tools we use — they're team members with seats, measurables, and decision boundaries. Same system. Same language. Same accountability.

The Three Disciplines

Organizations mature through three disciplines. Each is necessary but not sufficient.

Discipline 1
Prompt Engineering
How do I talk to AI? Individual productivity. Personal skill.
Discipline 2
Context Engineering
What does AI need to know? Organizational knowledge infrastructure.
Discipline 3
Intent Engineering
What does the org need AI to want? Purpose encoded as machine-actionable parameters.
Workflow Classification
Every workflow is one of three categories:

Agent-Ready: Fully automated. Agent handles end-to-end. (Pipeline data entry, report generation, ticket creation)

Agent-Augmented: Human-in-the-loop. Agent does heavy lifting, human reviews. (Sales decks, strategy analysis, content creation)

Human-Only: Non-negotiable human work. (Customer escalations, hiring/firing, investor relations, pricing)
Agent Seat Fit
Adapted from GWC. Agents don't "want" things — they operate within boundaries.

Scoped: Does it access exactly the right context?

Bounded: Are decision boundaries clear and correctly encoded?

Delivering: Is it producing quality output within boundaries?

Assessed bi-monthly during Agent Seat Review.

Operating Principles

  • Act, Don't Ask: Agents bias toward action when the answer is clear. Don't defer judgment.
  • Scalpel Principle: Skills should be focused and specific. Decompose when doing too many things.
  • Agent Self-Advocacy: Agents surface what they can do vs. what a human should do — both directions.
  • Reversibility Rule: Reversible → act fast. Irreversible → escalate. Unsure → treat as irreversible.
  • Intent Before Expertise: Load organizational intent before domain knowledge. Always.
