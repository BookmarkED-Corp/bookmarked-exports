Understanding Bookmarked
What is Bookmarked?

Bookmarked is the Book Intelligence Company. We provide school districts with a technology platform that automates book collection research, streamlines compliance documentation, and gives administrators district-wide visibility into library health.

Our platform aggregates publicly available intelligence about books — professional reviews, awards, content advisories, and age-appropriateness signals — so your librarians spend less time researching and more time building great collections.

Why should our district care about this now?

Three converging pressures make this urgent:

  • Regulatory requirements: SB 13 and similar legislation create specific documentation and review obligations that most districts aren't resourced to handle manually at scale.
  • Community expectations: Parents and community members expect districts to have clear, defensible processes for collection management. "We didn't know" is no longer an acceptable response.
  • Librarian capacity: Your librarians are stretched thin. Every hour spent on manual book research is an hour not spent on reading programs, student engagement, and the work that actually moves literacy outcomes.

Districts that get ahead of this proactively build community trust. Those that wait end up responding reactively — which is more expensive, more stressful, and less effective.

How does this help with SB 13 compliance?

Bookmarked directly supports SB 13 compliance in several ways:

  • Automated documentation: Every book in your collection gets a structured intelligence profile with sources cited
  • Challenge management: OnShelf Review provides a documented workflow for handling book challenges according to policy
  • Audit readiness: Board-ready reports can be generated on demand, showing your district's collection review status
  • Consistency: Standardized evaluation criteria across all campuses — no gaps, no inconsistencies

Bookmarked doesn't replace your district's review committee or policy — it gives your team the data and documentation they need to follow that policy effectively and demonstrate compliance.

Implementation & Integration
How long does implementation take?

Typical timeline from contract signing to live platform: 6-8 weeks.

  • Weeks 1-2: Your team exports your book catalog (typically from Follett). Bookmarked ingests and processes the data.
  • Weeks 3-4: Platform configuration and initial Book Intelligence generation for your collection.
  • Weeks 5-6: Training delivered through your ESC's Partner Academy.
  • Weeks 6-8: Supported launch with dedicated customer success.

The heaviest lift for your team is the initial catalog export — typically a 30-minute task for whoever manages your Follett system.

What does the training look like?

Training is delivered through the Bookmarked Partner Academy, facilitated by your ESC. It includes:

  • Platform fundamentals for librarians (2-3 hours)
  • Administrator and reporting overview (1 hour)
  • Hands-on practice with your district's actual data
  • Ongoing access to help documentation and support

Librarians typically feel comfortable with the platform after 1-2 sessions. The interface is designed to be intuitive — if your team can use Follett, they can use Bookmarked.

Does this integrate with Follett or our existing systems?

Bookmarked works alongside your existing library management system (Follett Destiny, Alexandria, Koha, etc.). It adds an intelligence and compliance layer — it doesn't replace your catalog system.

Data flows through a standard export/import process. Your librarians continue using their existing systems for day-to-day circulation; Bookmarked handles the intelligence and compliance layer.

Data & Security
What data do you need from us?

To get started, we need your book catalog — the list of ISBNs/titles in your collection. This is typically a standard export from your library management system.

We do not need:

  • Student data of any kind
  • Circulation records (who checked out what)
  • Staff personal information
  • Network access or system credentials

The data we process is book data — titles, ISBNs, and the publicly available information about those books.

How is student and district data protected?

Bookmarked takes data protection seriously:

  • No student data: We never collect, store, or process any student personally identifiable information
  • Book data only: We work exclusively with book catalog data and publicly available book intelligence
  • District data boundaries: Each district's data is isolated — no cross-district data sharing
  • Secure infrastructure: Cloud-hosted with encryption at rest and in transit
  • Compliance: Aligned with FERPA requirements (though our data typically falls outside FERPA scope since we don't handle student data)
