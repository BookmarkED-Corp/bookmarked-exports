Key Decisions from the Call

🎯 Training Economics Model

  • Initial training (2 sessions): Bookmarked PAYS the ESC to deliver onboarding training to districts
  • Ongoing PD: ESCs charge districts from professional development funds — ESCs keep 100% of PD revenue
  • Content ownership: Bookmarked owns all training content; ESCs get delivery rights + revenue
  • Why: We're not in the PD business. We get community + engagement + stickiness. They get revenue.
  • Tier structure: Small / Medium / Large — half-hour overview → intensive training → specific/customized sessions

📋 ESC Customization

Every ESC is different. Amanda at ESC 15 delivers PD differently than ESC 10. We build the framework and modules, then sit down with each ESC to customize delivery. Don't over-cook it — build it, show it, get feedback, iterate.

🔑 V2 is the Unlock

The biggest gap in the current pipeline is that older prospects haven't seen V2. Every asset we build needs to lead with "here's what's new, here's what problem this solves." The V2 product overview is the single most important asset.

💬 Community > Revenue

We're not trying to extract money from training. We're trying to create community — a place where ESCs and their districts engage with Bookmarked content, share best practices, and build loyalty. The ROI is retention and expansion, not training fees.

🔄 Three Versions of Everything

Every deliverable below gets built in 3 variations so Steve and Jan can pick what resonates best. Different messaging angles, different visual treatments, different emphasis. We choose the best, refine it, and ship it.
